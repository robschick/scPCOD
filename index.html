PCOD models need:

  • The number of times an animal responds to a disturbance in a year
  • The distribution of response severities

What is the Problem?

  • In order to run PCOD models, we need realistic estimates of exposure. However,
  • We lack exposure on individual animals at biologically realistic time frames (~1 year)
  • Current models (e.g. SafeSIMM, 3MB), fail to do this

What we plan to do

  • Produce realistic tracks while animals are on the range for a period of 1 year
  • Use this to estimate exposure
  • We not explicitly concerned with movements off the range, unless it influences movements back toward the range

What we plan to do, II

  • Fit inferential movement models to data from MarEcotel and Cascadia
  • Build an IBM to
    • spatially implicit, e.g. on/off-range
    • temporally explicit at daily resolution (for one year)

What we plan to do, III

  • In the IBM, while on range, explore 4 types of movements:
    1. No Movement
    2. Random
    3. Resource based movements
    4. Center of attraction/repulsion
  • Summarize Exposure
